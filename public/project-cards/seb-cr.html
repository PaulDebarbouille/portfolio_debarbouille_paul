Conversion Rate Analysis SEB Group (France)

Paradox solved: +45% traffic / -0.18pts conversion

📅 Jan - May 2025 · 6-month internship
👤 Solo · D2C Online Business Analyst
Qlik Sense SQL Google Analytics Excel (Analysis) PowerPoint

Context

SEB Group French e-commerce sites face a strange situation: traffic is booming (+45% visitors) but conversion rate is dropping (-0.18 points). Is this a real problem or just a normal effect of growth?

The goal: understand if this drop hides a real performance issue or if it is a math effect linked to growth. Then find concrete actions to turn this growing traffic into more sales.

Mission

Analyze 5 months of web data to understand why conversion rate is dropping despite traffic growth. Separate math effects from real problems. Present findings and recommendations to the Chief Marketing Officer.

Volume: Millions of sessions · Transactions · Page views Scope: Jan-May 2025 vs 2024 · France · 5 brands Type: Customer journeys · Traffic sources · Product catalog · Stock

Impact

Paradox solved: healthy growth confirmed 45% of the conversion drop explained by a math effect: the fastest-growing brand (A) has always had a lower conversion rate (0.35% vs 0.70-0.95% for others). No real performance problem.
Real improvement opportunities identified 2 product ranges get many views but few sales. Detailed analysis shows: pricing issues vs competition, no stock problems.
Strategic quick win: monetize recipe traffic Recipe pages are booming (+95% visits) but have no buy buttons. Recommendation: add direct purchase buttons. Brand A team already started product planning.
Deliverables: Executive presentation to CMO · Detailed report with all analysis for D2C online team

Approach

Investigation method in 7 steps

  • Find which brands drive growth: 3 out of 5 brands explain 100% of traffic growth → Focus analysis on these 3 brands
  • Calculate the math effect: Brand A (fastest growing) has always had a lower conversion rate. When it takes more share, the overall average drops mechanically → 45% of the drop explained
  • Analyze by traffic source: Created a "Responsibility Index" measuring impact of each channel (Google, Direct, etc.) on conversion → Direct and SEO channels bring lots of volume but with a slight conversion drop
  • Study visit quality: How many pages do visitors look at?
    • Direct traffic: more short visits (1-2 pages), which naturally convert less
    • SEO traffic: same pages per visit as before, but conversion still dropping
  • Analyze page types visited: Which pages attract traffic? → Recipe pages are booming (+95%) but you cannot buy there. Product pages are also up (+95%) which is good
  • Map high-performing vs underperforming products: Chart crossing "number of views" and "number of purchases" → 2 product ranges stand out: many views, few purchases
  • Deep dive on problem products: Zoom on specific items → Example: one product with +80% views but -2.8 points conversion, no stock issue, price dropped 15% but still not competitive

Feedback

Post-presentation impact: Following the CMO presentation, Brand A team started product planning to add purchase zones directly on recipe pages (currently 0% e-commerce functionality on this content type). This strategic recommendation aims to capitalize on the +95% recipe traffic growth.

Personal reflection: This project helped me develop a rigorous data investigation method in a real business context. Creating the "Responsibility Index" to measure multi-dimensional impact (brand × channel × conversion) was particularly helpful. Working independently with regular validation from my supervisor taught me how to structure a complex analysis step by step and adapt my communication to different audiences (operational teams vs CMO).

Key learning: The importance of separating mechanical effects (weighting, mix) from structural effects (traffic quality, product relevance) to avoid wrong conclusions and focus actions on real value drivers.
