Sales Performance Analysis Savencia (Europe)

€500K-€1M potential gain identified

📅 Nov 3-21, 2024 · 3 weeks
👥 Team of 3 · Leader & Business Analyst
Excel (Analysis) Python (Pandas, Data Cleaning) PowerPoint

Context

Savencia Food Service Premium sees that Italy and Spain are selling much less than France. The existing reports did not explain why.

The goal: use billing data to find out why some regions are underperforming, measure the gap, and suggest ways to fix it.

Mission

Analyze 2 years of sales data from multiple sources to find the root causes of poor performance (price, volume, product mix, promotions) and give clear recommendations.

Volume: 3M+ invoices · 300K clients · 20K products Scope: Jan 2023 - Oct 2025 · 3 European regions Type: B2B/B2C transactions · Product hierarchy · Buying behavior · Promotions

Impact

€500K-€1M potential gain (90% confidence) Clear recommendations for the Chocolate B category of the CacaoLuxe brand in Italy/Spain
70% of underperformance explained Sales shifted to low-price products: the cheapest tier went from 50% to 77% of Chocolate B sales
3 quick wins identified Train sales teams on upselling · Stop aggressive low-price campaigns · Add auto cross-sell online
Deliverable: Executive presentation with full breakdown · Root cause analysis by region · Prioritized action plan with financial estimates

Approach

Top-down analysis method

  • Revenue breakdown: Revenue = Number of clients × Frequency × Average price/kg × Average volume → Found that price/kg is the main driver (70% of growth in France vs negative in Italy/Spain)
  • Brand analysis: Price/volume contribution by brand → Found 2 different causes: (1) CacaoLuxe prices dropping in Italy/Spain but stable in France, (2) NoirEclat (premium brand) booming only in France
  • CacaoLuxe category drill-down: Breakdown by product range → The chocolate range is 100% responsible for the price drop in Italy/Spain
  • Price tier analysis: Sales distribution by price tier for Chocolate B and C
    • Chocolate B: massive shift to cheapest tier (50% → 77% of sales) = 70% of underperformance
    • Chocolate C: increase in cheapest tier due to weighting effect = 30% of underperformance, not a real problem
  • Root cause validation: Aggressive promotions on low-price Chocolate B + lack of upselling = eating into premium sales
  • Recommendations: 3 actionable quick wins with financial estimates and 90% confidence interval
