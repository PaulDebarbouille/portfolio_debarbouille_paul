-2% total emissions in 3 weeks
Sept-Oct 2024 · 3 weeks
Team of 3
Excel (Analysis) Python (Pandas, Data Cleaning, Seaborn) PowerPoint

Context

Procurement (scope 3.1) represents 33% of SEB's total carbon footprint. Between 2021 and 2024, carbon emissions increased, jeopardizing the SBTi 2030 target of -25% vs 2021. The procurement team had detailed historical data but lacked actionable insights.

Mission

Analyze 4 years of procurement data to identify priority levers for carbon emission reduction and formulate concrete recommendations to achieve the SBTi 2030 target.

Volume: 1M+ rows · 20 columns Scope: 2021-2024 (Worldwide) Type: Purchase volumes, emission factors, product categories, regions, recycled/virgin status

Impact

-2% total procurement emissions Achievable impact in less than one quarter
-10% of rising emissions neutralized Targeted action on 2 product categories in specific regions
Actionable lever validated Recommendation: rework recycled/virgin mix on identified categories
Deliverable: Executive presentation with prioritized and quantified recommendations, validated by the procurement team

Approach

Progressive Top-Down Methodology

  • Strategic segmentation: Focus on 80% of emissions controllable by the procurement team
  • Region × category cross-analysis: Isolation of 5 categories in specific geographic zones = 80% of the increase
  • Variation breakdown: Calculation of the respective contribution of:
    • Increase in purchase volumes
    • Evolution of emission factor per kg
  • Actionable lever isolation: 2/5 categories show an increase linked to emission factor (+30%) → actionable lever
  • Root cause analysis: Significant decrease in the proportion of recycled products in these 2 categories
