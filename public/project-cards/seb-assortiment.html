Context

SEB Group launches a new product family (High-performance Blenders) on its D2C European sites. Strategic question: will this new range eat into the existing Traditional Mixers family or create additional growth?

The goal: measure Traditional Mixers performance over 12 months, assess the real impact of High-performance Blenders launch, and find growth opportunities by geographic market (Northern, Central/Eastern, Eastern Europe).

Mission

Analyze 12 months of e-commerce data to find the drivers of Traditional Mixers growth, detect any cannibalization effects with High-performance Blenders, and give recommendations for local commercial teams.

Volume: Transactions · Product traffic · Prices · Stock · Margins Scope: Rolling 12 months · Greater Europe · 3 geographic zones Type: Product performance · Traffic sources · Pricing · Availability

Impact

Healthy growth confirmed: +28% over 12 months Performance maintained after High-performance Blenders launch (+26% post-launch). Zero cannibalization: both families complement each other and create additional growth.
Strong resilience despite changing product offer Balanced growth driven by well-managed innovations and solid existing products. No warning signs: positive commercial momentum supported by strong basics.
Strategic recommendation: capitalize on complementarity Keep aggressive pricing strategy + optimize stock to maximize momentum. Healthy Mixers/Blenders coexistence = opportunity for future planning and long-term profitability.
Deliverables: Detailed report for D2C Europe teams · Mixers/Blenders cannibalization analysis · Pricing and stock recommendations by geographic zone

Approach

Structured analysis method in 8 steps

  • Check for cannibalization: Compare Mixers growth before/after Blenders launch → Performance stable (+28% before, +26% after). 3-year analysis confirms: no slowdown, traffic actually accelerating
  • Find high-performing geographic zones: Breakdown by European region → 2 zones account for 95% of growth, allowing targeted commercial actions
  • Break down growth sources: Cascade analysis to understand where growth comes from
    • Traffic: main driver (+75% contribution)
    • Conversion: significant improvement (+18% contribution)
    • Average basket: voluntary decrease to be more competitive (-10% contribution)
  • Find products driving growth: Analysis of 8 key products → New products vs existing products have different but complementary strategies
  • Analyze marketing channels by product: Map traffic sources (Google, organic SEO, Display, etc.)
    • New products: strong natural visibility on Google (SEO) + Display campaigns
    • Existing products: maintained advertising investment (Google Ads, Paid)
  • Analyze price competitiveness: Found 2 star products with price drop of -5/-6% → Check if positioning vs competition is optimal
  • Focus on existing products only: Isolate products present before 2023 to remove "newness" effect → Growth driven 95% by conversion improvement (+0.5pts) thanks to better prices and fewer stock-outs
  • Validate profitability: Calculate margin impact per product → Identified items maintaining profitability despite price drops. Recommendation: be even more aggressive on price for top-margin item

Feedback

Business impact: This analysis reassured commercial teams about no cannibalization between the two product families. Recommendations (aggressive pricing + stock optimization in high-performing zones) were integrated into the D2C Greater Europe strategy.

Personal reflection: This project taught me to conduct a "defensive" analysis (prove there is no problem) just as rigorously as an "offensive" analysis (find opportunities). Creating multi-dimensional responsibility indexes (geography × product × channel × KPI) allowed me to precisely measure each lever contribution, making it easier for local teams to make decisions.

Key learning: The importance of a 3+ year historical analysis to establish a trend baseline and distinguish cyclical effects from structural effects. Without this long-term perspective, the conclusion on cannibalization could have been wrong.
